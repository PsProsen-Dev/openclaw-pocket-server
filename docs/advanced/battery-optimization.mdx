# OEM-Specific Battery Fixes

Android OEMs (Original Equipment Manufacturers) heavily modify the Android kernel to improve battery life. They do this by aggressively killing background apps. You must disable these optimizations for Termux.

Find your phone brand below and apply the fixes.

---

## Xiaomi / POCO / Redmi (MIUI & HyperOS)

Xiaomi is notoriously aggressive with background apps.

1. Go to **Settings** > **Apps** > **Manage Apps** > **Termux**
2. Enable **AutoStart**
3. Go to **Battery saver** and set it to **No restrictions**
4. Go back to Termux app info, tap **Permissions**, and ensure **Display pop-up windows while running in the background** is allowed
5. Open the Recents (multitasking) menu, long press on Termux, and tap the **Lock** icon to lock it in RAM.

## OPPO / OnePlus / Realme (ColorOS & OxygenOS)

1. Go to **Settings** > **Battery** > **Advanced settings** > **Optimize battery use**
2. Find Termux and set to **Don't optimize**
3. Go to **Settings** > **Apps** > **App management** > **Termux**
4. Tap **Battery usage** and enable:
   - Allow foreground activity
   - Allow background activity
   - Allow auto-launch
5. Lock the app in the Recents menu.

## Vivo / iQOO (FuntouchOS & OriginOS)

1. Go to **Settings** > **Battery** > **Background power consumption management**
2. Find Termux and select **Don't restrict background power usage**
3. Go to **Settings** > **Apps & permissions** > **Permission management** > **Autostart**
4. Enable autostart for Termux
5. Lock the app in the Recents menu.

## Samsung (One UI)

Samsung is generally better, but still requires tweaking.

1. Go to **Settings** > **Apps** > **Termux** > **Battery**
2. Select **Unrestricted**
3. Go to **Settings** > **Device Care** > **Battery** > **Background usage limits**
4. Ensure Termux is NOT in the "Sleeping apps" or "Deep sleeping apps" lists
5. Tap **Never sleeping apps** and add Termux.
